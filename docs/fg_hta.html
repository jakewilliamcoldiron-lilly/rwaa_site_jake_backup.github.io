HTA - Health Technology Assessment

Supporting payer and regulatory evidence submissions including indirect treatment comparisons, network meta-analyses, systematic reviews, and value dossier statistical inputs.

HTA — Additional Access

HTA analysts working with clinical trial data need CLUWE access to specific compound/study folders (Step 2).

Ask your Lilly Lead for the exact CLUWE path.

Vault Clinical / eTMF Access

Lilly FTE

ServiceNow → “eTMF account request”. Lilly Lead can initiate. Accounts created within 1–2 business days.

Vendor / Contractor

ServiceNow → More → Accounts and Passwords → Application Accounts and Access → eTMF account request. Vendor or Lilly Lead can submit. 1–2 business days.

HTA Learning Progression Guide

The following resources are drawn from the SDnA Role-Based Learning Progression Guide (LPG) for HTA. Work through these with your supervisor or coach to build foundational knowledge in health technology assessment and economic modelling.

Month One — HTA Introduction

Topic Details Comments Resources
Review resources for market access introduction Meet with Supervisor/Coach to identify relevant introductory materials
Select resources based on assigned projects Meet with Supervisor/Coach to tailor learning to your project scope
HTA Toolkit The HTA Toolkit summarises the clinical, humanistic, and economic body of evidence for a Lilly compound and relevant comparators. Supervisor/coach to assess which material is relevant depending on the stage of launch of the compound GPORWE · Introduction to Dossiers and HTAs #9100995

Month Two — Economic Modelling

Topic Details Curriculum Comments Resources
Economic modelling onboarding Series of seminars developed by GPORWE as a high-level introduction to the topic DEV-HO: RWE 301-MOD: Working with Health Economic Models Extensive training available in the GPORWE Learning and Development link. Supervisor/coach to assess what material is relevant for the employee. Economic Modeling Onboarding
What are utilities? Review this video with your coach to better understand an important component of economic modelling Utilities in Cost-Utility Analysis (CUA)
Economic modelling in practice Discuss with your supervisor/coach what literature or previous models to review. Depending on the type of support, review adaptations and country/payer requirements (such as ICER or NICE). Find relevant economic model for the same or similar indication/therapeutic area The Elk Test
