Why CMH Matters to RWAA

Cardiometabolic Health (CMH) is one of the highest-volume and highest-priority Therapeutic Areas for real-world analytics at Lilly. The combination of large, well-established patient populations, mature real-world data (RWD) sources, and intense payer and HTA scrutiny makes CMH a primary driver of RWAA’s workload and strategic impact.

RWAA supports CMH across the full evidence lifecycle — from early pipeline characterization of treatment patterns and unmet need, through post-approval effectiveness and safety studies, to ongoing payer and HTA submissions.

Disease Areas & RWD Landscape

Type 2 Diabetes (T2D)

T2D is one of the best-characterized disease areas in real-world data. Large, longitudinal claims and EHR datasets provide rich opportunity for treatment pattern analyses, adherence studies, and comparative effectiveness research.

Key RWD considerations:

  • Claims data (Optum, MarketScan, IQVIA): Strong for treatment patterns, adherence, switching, and CV event rates
  • EHR data (Optum EHR, TriNetX): Adds lab values (A1C, eGFR, lipids) critical for endpoint ascertainment
  • Registry data: Useful for long-term outcomes and patient-reported data
  • Linkage opportunities: CMS Medicare/Medicaid linkages important for older and dual-eligible populations

Obesity

Obesity as a standalone indication is a newer RWD challenge. Diagnosis codes are historically undercoded in claims — BMI-based cohort identification using EHR data is often preferred over ICD-10 codes alone.

Key RWD considerations:

  • BMI and weight trajectories require EHR or registry sources; claims alone are insufficient
  • Treatment discontinuation and regain patterns are high-priority for HEOR and payers
  • Comorbidity burden (T2D, HFpEF, OSA, MASH) is critical for characterizing the treated population
  • Weight loss as a primary endpoint requires careful validation in RWD settings

Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) & Heart Failure

CV outcomes are among the most important — and most complex — endpoints to ascertain from RWD. MACE (major adverse cardiovascular events) and HHF (hospitalization for heart failure) are common primary endpoints requiring validated algorithms.

Key RWD considerations:

  • MACE ascertainment: Validated ICD-10 algorithms exist but require careful adaptation by data source
  • HFpEF vs HFrEF differentiation: Challenging in claims; EHR + echocardiogram data often needed
  • Cardiovascular event adjudication: May be required for regulatory-grade RWE studies

Key Data Sources for CMH

Claims Databases

  • Optum Clinformatics — large commercially insured + Medicare Advantage population; good for CV events
  • IBM MarketScan — employer-sponsored insurance; useful for working-age T2D population
  • IQVIA PharMetrics Plus — broad commercial; strong pharmacy linkage
  • CMS Medicare / Medicaid — essential for elderly and low-income populations with high cardiometabolic burden

EHR Databases

  • Optum EHR — large de-identified EHR; includes labs (A1C, BMI, lipids), vital signs, structured notes
  • TriNetX — federated EHR network; useful for feasibility and rapid cohort sizing
  • Flatiron — oncology-focused but used for comorbidity analyses in CMH/oncology overlap patients

Specialty / Linked Sources

  • PINNACLE Registry (ACC) — cardiovascular registry with clinical depth for HF and CVD
  • Linked Claims + EHR — preferred design when lab data or weight is needed alongside utilization/cost
Data Source Selection Tip

For CMH studies requiring both clinical endpoints (A1C, BMI, eGFR) and utilization/cost outcomes, linked claims-EHR datasets are strongly preferred. Pure claims studies are adequate for treatment patterns and event-based endpoints with validated algorithms.

Analytic Considerations

Cohort Design

  • New-user active comparator design is the gold standard for CER in CMH; reduces channeling and indication bias
  • T2D cohort entry is typically anchored to first dispensing of index therapy with a washout period (commonly 6–12 months)
  • Obesity cohorts require careful BMI eligibility criteria; consider sensitivity analyses with and without diagnosis code inclusion

Confounding Control

  • CMH populations have high comorbidity burden — propensity score methods (IPTW, matching) are standard
  • High-dimensional propensity scores (hdPS) are used when variable lists are large
  • Disease severity proxies (prior A1C levels, insulin use, prior CV events) are critical covariates

Endpoint Ascertainment

  • MACE and HHF algorithms should be pre-specified and cite published validation literature
  • Hypoglycemia ascertainment in claims is known to be underestimated — sensitivity analyses recommended
  • Weight change as an endpoint requires EHR or linked data; cannot be derived from claims alone

Regulatory-Grade RWE

RWAA is increasingly involved in studies intended to support or supplement regulatory submissions. For CMH assets (particularly tirzepatide indications), this means:

  • Pre-specified statistical analysis plans (SAPs)
  • Transparent protocol registration (e.g., EU PAS Register, ClinicalTrials.gov)
  • Adherence to FDA RWE frameworks and EMA guidance on RWD/RWE
